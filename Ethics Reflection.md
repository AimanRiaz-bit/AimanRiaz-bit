# Ethics Reflection

## Ethical Issue Chosen

An important ethical concern in modern software development is the effect 
of social media platforms on users' mental health. Many platforms are 
intentionally designed to maximize user engagement, which may be good for 
business, but can lead to addictive usage habits and, over time, 
negatively impact the mental well-being of users.

## Problem Explanation

The main issue is the use of algorithmic systems that are built to keep 
users on the platform for as long as possible. While this works well from 
a business point of view, these systems have been connected to increased 
anxiety, stress, low self-esteem, and reduced productivity, especially 
among younger users. The constant presence of perfect and idealized 
content also encourages unhealthy comparisons, which adds to the 
psychological harm on vulnerable individuals.

## Relevant Professional Code

This issue is directly related to the ACM Code of Ethics, which states 
that computing professionals should:

- Avoid causing harm to users and the general public
- Design systems with honest consideration for user well-being
- Support transparency and the responsible use of technology

## My Decision & Justification

As a future software developer, I strongly believe that user well-being 
should always come before engagement-focused design. Focusing only on 
time-on-platform at the cost of mental health is not ethically acceptable. 
Responsible developers should include features like usage tracking, screen 
time limits, content warnings, and clear algorithmic practices, not as 
extra additions, but as core parts of the design process.

## Conclusion

Ethical responsibility is not a side topic in software development, it is 
at the heart of it. Applications that affect human behavior have a duty to 
protect, not harm, the well-being of their users. As computing 
professionals, we must make sure that the systems we build serve people 
with honesty, care, and a true commitment to doing no harm.
